Flowers photos

Flowers are the reproductive structures of flowering plants and have many parts, including: Sepals: The outermost whorl of the flower, which protects the unopened bud. Sepals are usually green and resemble leaves. Petals: The second whorl of the flower, which attracts pollinators. Petals are usually brightly colored. Stamens: The male reproductive structures, which contain pollen grains. Pistils: The female reproductive structures, which contain ovules. Ovules: Contain the female gamete of the plant, known as the ovum or egg. Pollen: The food of pollinators, such as bees, wasps, beetles, and flies. Pollen grains can look different between flower types. Nectar: A food source for pollinators.

Roses pictures

Rose petals and hips contain vitamins A, B1, B2, B3, B6, C, E, and K, as well as folic acid, potassium, calcium, iron, tannin, and enzymes. Rose flowers are also rich in biologically active compounds like phenolics, flavonoids, anthocyanins, and carotenoids.
